## Who I Am

I am **Mehdi Hosseini**, a backend engineer and Computer Science Master's student at the **University of Tübingen**. I have spent the past five years building and shipping real software — not as a team member inheriting a codebase, but as a **co-founder** who owned the engineering decisions, the infrastructure, and the consequences when things broke.

I designed REST APIs, managed relational and non-relational databases in production, built containerized deployments with Docker, and learned how systems fail by being the person who had to fix them. That experience shapes how I think about software: **correctness, reliability, and maintainability** are not afterthoughts.

My current work at **Robert Bosch GmbH** puts me in a real production environment at one of the world's largest industrial companies, working on SQL-driven quality management systems. My time at **Deutsche Bundesbank** gave me exposure to the kind of institution where data correctness is non-negotiable.

I am now deepening my theoretical and systems foundations through my Master's degree — currently researching recursive query execution in DuckDB and working on AI and information retrieval projects.
